Kidney disease is a medical condition that occurs when the kidneys fail to function properly. It can be caused by a range of factors such as diabetes, hypertension, infections, and autoimmune disorders.   Kidney disease is a condition where the kidneys are damaged and cannot function properly. Some common causes of kidney disease include high blood pressure, diabetes, and inflammation of the kidneys. Symptoms can vary depending on the stage of the disease, but can include fatigue, nausea, swelling, and decreased urine output. Early detection and treatment of kidney disease can help prevent further damage to the kidneys and potentially delay the need for dialysis or a kidney transplant.
