Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how the body uses glucose, a type of sugar, as its main source of energy. When the body does not produce enough insulin, a hormone that regulates glucose levels, or when the body is unable to use insulin effectively, it can lead to high levels of glucose in the blood, which can cause a variety of health problems over time.


  The application of predicting diabetes can help in early detection and management of the disease. Machine learning algorithms can analyze various factors such as age, gender, family history, weight, and lifestyle habits to predict the risk of developing diabetes. This can aid in creating personalized prevention and treatment plans, as well as identifying individuals who may need more frequent checkups or monitoring. Additionally, diabetes prediction models can be used in healthcare research to identify trends and risk factors associated with the disease.
